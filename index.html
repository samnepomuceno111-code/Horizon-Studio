A Conversation with the Ocean

The ocean is an honest conversation, a raw and shifting tide that holds every dream I have ever been too afraid to speak out loud.

Beyond the Shores

I used to be afraid of the ocean. It was this vast, crushing weight that felt far too large for me to even try to understand. Even though I loved being on the beach, watching the golden light dance on the water and feeling the rhythmic push of the waves against my feet, I still feared it. I loved the idea of it from a distance, but the actual reality of the depths? That scared me. To me, the water was just the unknown, a place where you could lose your footing and be swept away by things you couldn't see.


A couple of years later, everything changed when I met someone whose favorite thing in the world is the sea. She doesn’t look at the water with hesitation; she looks at it like she belongs there. She loves everything about it, from the way the crashing yet soothing waves speak a language of their own to the sharp, refreshing scent of salt in the air. She saw beauty where I only saw danger. Over time, I started to see the world through her eyes. We'd talk for hours, then days, then months, and now it’s been over two years of her reshaping my entire perspective.


I wasn't a fan of many things but she gave me a new perspective. I hated the saying, “it’s going to be okay.” Too much uncertainty and too much on the line. But when it came to her, everything and I mean everything changed. I realized that saying, “everything’s going to be okay” is some sort of promise. like you’ll do everything in your power to make things okay. To help them through it. So every single time I say it, I mean it with every atom in my body.


Because of her, I love the ocean now. Not just the concept or the aesthetic, but the actual, physical reality of it. I've grown to love how mysterious it is. There's something vv comforting about realizing how small we are compared to the tides. It's a reminder that our problems are usually just ripples in a much larger, more beautiful existence. My favorite animal even became the whale shark because of her. Those gentle giants are exactly what she taught me: that something can be massive and powerful, yet incredibly peaceful and kind.


I’m sure now that if I ever had to run away, I’d run away with her. I wouldn’t even mind if the waves were to consume me, as long as I’m by her side. I think I’d even let it slide, all those corny jokes she makes and laughs at. My love for her is honestly through the roof. I can say for certain now that my life isn't, and won't be, wasted as long as she exists. With her around, I don’t feel the need to hold my breath or feel uncomfortable anymore; I can finally just breathe.


That transformation started because of one person. She is my anchor and my reason. When the world feels like it’s spinning too fast or the waves of life get too high to climb, she’s the one who keeps me steady. I made this digital page not just because of the water, but because I love the person who made me love the ocean. I wanted a canvas that captured the blue of the sea and the warmth of the sun, reflecting all the light she brought into my world.


I dedicate this to her. She's the one who has my heart and the anchor that grounds me. This page is a tribute to her and a record of how one person can take your greatest fear and turn it into your greatest paradise. She taught me that to love is to dive in, even when you’re afraid, because the reality of the deep is far more beautiful than the safety of the shore. It's her and only her, forever and always.
